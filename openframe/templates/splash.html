openframe alpha 0.1 is a platform for displaying art

Openframe is an open source platform for artists, developers, curators, and everyone else to explore new ways of consuming and interacting with art in our personal environment.

Openframe is free. Anyone can set up a frame using a display and a Raspberry Pi, and following these simple instructions.

Openframe is a collaborative, on-going project. Here's where we are:

  • Display content from the internet, or your own*
  • Save art to your collection
  • Mirror someone else's frame
  • See more
  • Add multiple users to a frame
  • Have multiple frames per user
  • See how many people are mirroring your frame / following your channel
  • Use tags to describe and find art
  • Chrome extension to save/display content
  • Settings for putting your frame to sleep

*Currently supporting images (jpeg and png's). Other types of content coming soon (websites, gifs, videos, Processing sketches, shaders, etc.).

Questions and answers.

Why this project?

A while back we started seeing a number of commercial projects that aimed to bring artwork from the internet into our homes. We'd been thinking along these lines too, but were interested in seeing what could happen if we put the power fully in the hands of the artists, programmers, and art viewers.

We wanted to build an open source platform that could be modified and extended to support new media types and sensor-based artwork, and that could be easily set up using a range of inexpensive hardware (Raspberry Pi, Odroid, etc.). With Openframe, we wanted to create a conduit through which an audience could experience and appreciate art in new ways, and which would allow artists to give new life to their work.

What's the future of Openframe?

We don't know. We're just starting, but we're excited about the potential.

We imagine a kid creating a drawing on an iPad and next day it's being displayed in the living rooms of four strangers who liked the piece. We imagine a small gallery curating a week of artwork for your home. We imagine an artist pushing some work in progress to their followers. We imagine an historian creating a collection of old hand-drawn maps. We imagine art that reacts to the local weather or the light in the room. More than anything, we imagine people using the platform in ways we... well... couldn't imagine :).

What artwork can I display?

The Openframe platform doesn't care what artwork you display; no content is stored on the Openframe server. It's up to you to make sure you have the permission of the content owner to display the work.

Also — for the moment, only images can be displayed, but that will soon be changing.
